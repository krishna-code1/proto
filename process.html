Process Explanation

1

Raw Material Preparation

Castor oil (vegetable oil), methanol (alcohol), and NaOH (sodium hydroxide catalyst) are measured and prepared in precise ratios for the transesterification reaction.

2

Ultrasonication Mixing

The raw materials are mixed using ultrasonic waves, which create intense cavitation bubbles that enhance the reaction rate and ensure thorough mixing at the molecular level.

3

Controlled Heating

The mixture is heated to 60¬∞C to accelerate the transesterification reaction where triglycerides in the oil react with methanol to form biodiesel and glycerol.

4

Gravity Separation

The reaction mixture settles, allowing the denser glycerol to separate and sink to the bottom while the lighter crude biodiesel rises to the top.

5

Glycerol Collection

Glycerol (glycerin) is drained off as a valuable byproduct that can be sold to soap manufacturers and other industries, making the process economically viable.

6

Water Washing

The crude biodiesel is washed with water to remove residual methanol, catalyst, soaps, and other impurities. This step is crucial for fuel quality.

7

Final Separation & Drying

Water is separated from the washed biodiesel through settling. The biodiesel may be heated gently to remove any remaining moisture.

8

Pure Biodiesel (B100)

The final product is pure biodiesel (B100) meeting ASTM D6751 standards, ready for use in diesel engines or for blending with petroleum diesel.
